Around Cedar Nest

Places to visit
in Sainj Valley

Sainj is Himachal's best-kept secret. Cedar Nest puts you at its heart — minutes from ancient meadows, hidden waterfalls and a UNESCO sanctuary.

01

Sacred Meadow · 2200m

Shangarh
Meadow

One of the most breathtaking alpine meadows in Himachal, surrounded by towering deodar cedars with a centuries-old wooden temple at its heart. Guests wake early for sunrise here, when mist clings to the cedars and the Great Himalayan National Park ridge rises dramatically behind.

○ 12 km from Cedar Nest · 35 min drive

02

Hidden Cascade

Rupi Raila
Waterfall

Hidden deep in a mossy gorge carved by millennia of glacial melt, Rupi Raila is the waterfall locals rarely share with outsiders. The walk through forest and boulder-strewn stream beds is itself a meditation. Cedar Nest's guides know the trail intimately.

○ 8 km · 25 min drive + 45 min trek

03

UNESCO World Heritage Buffer Zone

Sainj Wildlife
Sanctuary

Home to the Himalayan tahr, snow leopard, brown bear, and over 220 bird species. Enter on foot directly from the Cedar Nest property — the wilderness begins at your doorstep.

○ Adjacent to Cedar Nest · Enter on foot

Our Story

A Love Letter
to the Himalayas.

"Dear Sainj Valley, we first met you on an October morning some years back. It still feels like yesterday. That morning changed our lives — and yours."

This small but heartfelt declaration is the opening of a much longer love story — one that began with a dream, a valley, and a handful of ancient cedar trees. Cedar Nest exists because some places are too beautiful to keep to oneself.

Built with reverence for the landscape of Himachal Pradesh, every cabin is handcrafted from local deodar wood, perfectly placed for day trips to Shangarh Meadow, Rupi Raila Waterfall, and Pundrik Lake.
